About the Project

For our project, we examined the MBTA (Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority) dataset for Rail or "T" Ridership by Time Period, Line, and Station. The "T" system consists of four main lines: The Red line, going from Alewife in Cambridge down to Ashmont and Braintree in the South Shore, The Blue line, going from Bowdoin in the West end, to Wonderland in Revere; The Orange line, from Oak Grove in Malden, to Forest Hills in Jamaica Plain; and the Green line - a four-branched system starting from Lechmere in Cambridge, to Boston College (B), Cleveland Circle (C), Riverside (D), and Heath St. (E). As Boston-based students who frequently use the "T", we wanted to gain a better understanding of the transit system's traffic patterns across different times and locations.

We aim to uncover trends in station usage and identify possible mismatches between transit demand and service availability. By doing so, we hope to highlight where improvements could be made to better serve the diverse communities that rely on public transit in the city.

Data Source

To support this analysis, we're working with MBTA rail ridership data from MassDOT's public portal. The dataset covers three seasonal snapshots (2017, 2018, and 2019) and includes features such as station names, boarding, alighting, and total flow counts, number of service days, and other general route information. In total, it contains over 7,900 entries across 17 attributes.

Research Questions

How do geographic and temporal factors shape ridership patterns on the MBTA?

  • Are some stations overburdened while others are underutilized?
  • Do current service levels meet the actual needs of the communities they're intended to serve?
  • How can we make the "T" more efficient for its users?

Project Scope

For this project, we've chosen to focus on MBTA heavy and light rail lines, leaving out commuter rail and bus networks to keep the scope manageable and the analysis consistent. By zeroing in on three years of subway data, we can capture year-to-year changes while maintaining clarity in our results.

The final product includes a mix of static charts and interactive maps, allowing users to explore the data from both broad and detailed perspectives.
