MBTA Stacked Area Chart

Note: This interactive stacked area chart visualizes MBTA ridership trends over time, categorized by transit line. It allows users to explore variations in total boardings across different time periods and identify peak ridership trends across routes.

About This Visualization

This stacked area chart shows how total boardings are distributed across MBTA stations, broken down by subway line and time of day. Each section of color represents a different transit line—Red, Orange, Green, or Blue—making it easy to see how different routes contribute to overall ridership. Taller stacks indicate higher boardings at particular stations, helping to spotlight the busiest stops in the system. By adjusting the filters, users can explore trends for specific lines and time periods to better understand where and when ridership peaks. This visualization offers valuable insight for transit planners and riders alike, supporting decisions about service planning and travel timing.

The visualization breaks down ridership into the following time periods:

Key Insights

Peak Ridership Trends

The highest ridership levels are observed during AM_PEAK and PM_PEAK hours, with significant variations by line.

Route Comparisons

The Red Line consistently records the highest passenger volume, while other lines show distinct ridership patterns based on time of day.

Implications

These insights can inform transit planning and policy decisions, such as:

  • Adjusting train frequencies to accommodate peak demand periods
  • Enhancing infrastructure at high-traffic stations
  • Optimizing schedules based on station-specific ridership trends
