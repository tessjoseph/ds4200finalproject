Average Flow by Route and Time of Day

Note: This visualization shows ridership patterns across all MBTA subway lines throughout different time periods of the day. Each line is color-coded to match the actual MBTA line colors.

About This Visualization

This line plot illustrates the average daily total flow for each MBTA subway line across different times of the day. Each line corresponds to an MBTA subway line, color-coded to match its official route color:

Red Line
Orange Line
Green Line
Blue Line

Time Periods

The x-axis represents different time periods throughout the day:

VERY_EARLY_MORNING Before early commuters
EARLY_AM Early morning hours
AM_PEAK Morning rush hour
MIDDAY_BASE Late morning hours
MIDDAY_SCHOOL School dismissal times
PM_PEAK Evening rush hour
EVENING Early evening hours
LATE_EVENING Late evening hours
NIGHT Night hours

The y-axis measures average flow, capturing total activity (boardings and alightings) at each station.

Key Insights

Peak Hour Patterns

The plot reveals distinct peaks during the AM_PEAK and PM_PEAK periods, aligning with the traditional rush hours for commuters traveling to and from work or school. Outside of these peak periods, ridership declines, with the lowest activity during early morning and late-night hours. This pattern strongly suggests that the MBTA primarily serves commuters traveling to work and school. Note: This data only includes Mon-Fri, as weekends have significantly less ridership, and differences in traffic patterns.

Line Popularity

The Red and Orange Lines consistently show the highest levels of ridership, indicating heavier transit demand in areas such as Cambridge, Somerville, Malden, Jamaica Plain, and the South Shore. The Blue Line follows, with lower ridership levels, expected given its shorter route length compared to the other lines.

Surprising Finding

The Green Line exhibits the lowest overall flow despite having the most stations and multiple branches. This suggests that ridership is more dispersed across Newton, Brookline, and Medford, rather than concentrated at a few high-traffic stations.

Implications

By highlighting these trends, this visualization provides valuable insights into how transit demand fluctuates throughout the day and across different regions, offering a clearer picture of where service improvements or adjustments may be needed.

  • Transit authorities could adjust train frequencies during peak hours on the Red and Orange lines
  • Investigate why the Green Line sees lower ridership despite having the most stations
  • Consider optimizations for early morning and late evening service based on actual demand
