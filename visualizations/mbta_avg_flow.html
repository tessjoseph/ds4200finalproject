MBTA Station Activity Heatmap

Note: This interactive heatmap visualizes ridership patterns across MBTA stations during different time periods. Explore the data by interacting with the map.

About This Visualization

This heatmap shows the average daily passenger flow for each MBTA station across different time periods. The visualization helps identify which stations experience the highest activity levels and when these peak periods occur.

Key Insights

High-Traffic Stations

Downtown Crossing, Park Street, and South Station consistently show the highest activity levels across all time periods, reflecting their status as major transfer points and central locations.

Temporal Patterns

Most stations exhibit clear peaks during AM_PEAK and PM_PEAK hours, with significantly reduced activity during early morning and late night periods. This pattern is especially pronounced at stations near business districts.

Line Comparison

Red Line stations generally show higher average flow compared to other lines, particularly at key locations like Harvard, Porter, and Davis. Green Line stations display more evenly distributed ridership across their routes.

Implications

Understanding station-specific activity patterns allows transit planners to target capacity improvements where they're most needed and adjust service schedules to better align with actual usage patterns.

  • High-traffic transfer stations would benefit from expanded platforms and improved passenger flow systems
  • Stations with dramatic peaks could be candidates for express service during rush hours
  • Understanding neighborhood-specific patterns can inform targeted service improvements
