MBTA Station Sankey Diagram

Note: This interactive Sankey diagram visualizes ridership flow between different MBTA stations. You can explore the data by interacting with the diagram.

About This Visualization

This Sankey diagram illustrates the flow of passengers between different MBTA stations during various time periods. It provides an overview of station connectivity and relative ridership movements, showing the intensity of flows across the network.

Key Insights

High-Traffic Stations

Stations such as Downtown Crossing, Park Street, and South Station exhibit high passenger flow across multiple lines, reflecting their roles as major transfer points.

Temporal Patterns

The Sankey diagram highlights the dominant flow during peak hours, with stations experiencing heavy movement during the AM and PM rush hours.

Line Comparison

The diagram helps compare ridership across the Red, Orange, Green, and Blue lines, illustrating where passengers tend to transfer most frequently.

Implications

This Sankey diagram assists in identifying key patterns that can inform decisions on service optimizations, station upgrades, and capacity improvements.

  • Transfer-heavy stations may need expanded facilities to handle high traffic
  • Peak flow periods can help design better schedules and express services
  • Frequent transfer points can serve as hubs for strategic service planning
